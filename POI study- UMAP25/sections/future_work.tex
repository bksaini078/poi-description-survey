\section{Future Work}
Future research directions include expanding the study to include more diverse participants, such as older adults and people from different cultural backgrounds. Such expansion would help clarify how age and culture affect user acceptance of AI-generated travel content. Beyond short-term surveys, longitudinal field studies with real travel platforms should be conducted to track actual user behavior, including booking rates and long-term trust. Additionally, more detailed personalization factors such as trip purpose, travel companions, and budget constraints could be tested to examine their effects on user satisfaction.

A second research direction involves the transparency and integration of AI-generated descriptions. Investigation is needed into how explaining why certain information was highlighted can improve user trust. It is also important to study the potential for filter bubbles where over-personalization might limit new and surprising discoveries. Methods that balance personalized relevance with novelty and diversity should be developed. Finally, the integration of adaptive descriptions into existing recommendation systems warrants exploration, particularly to support users from the start of their search until booking completion.
