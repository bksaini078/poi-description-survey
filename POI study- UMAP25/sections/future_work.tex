\section{Future Work}
Future research will first expand the study to include more diverse participants, such as older adults and people from different cultural backgrounds. This will help us understand how age and culture affect how users accept AI-generated travel content. We also plan to move beyond short-term surveys by conducting longitudinal field studies with real travel platforms. These studies will track actual user behavior, including booking rates and long-term trust. Additionally, we will test more detailed personalization by adding factors like trip purpose, travel companions, and budget constraints to see how they affect user satisfaction.

A second research direction involves the transparency and integration of these AI descriptions. We will investigate how explaining why certain information was highlighted can improve user trust. It is also important to study the potential for filter bubbles where over-personalization might limit new and surprising discoveries. We plan to develop methods that balance personalized relevance with novelty and diversity. Finally, we will explore how these adaptive descriptions can be integrated into existing recommendation systems to support users from the start of their search until they complete a booking.
