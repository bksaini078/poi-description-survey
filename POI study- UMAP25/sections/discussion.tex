\section{Discussion}
This study examined whether LLM-generated personalization of POI descriptions improves user engagement and perceived information quality without undermining trust. Overall, AI-adapted descriptions were rated as clearer than manual descriptions (significant improvement; $p = 0.0218$) and showed comparable trustworthiness with a marginal improvement in trust ratings ($p = 0.050$). Engagement preferences were essentially unchanged across conditions (AI: 221/510, 43.3\%; manual: 220/510, 43.1\%; equal: 69/510, 13.5\%), suggesting that personalization can enhance perceived quality without reducing willingness to engage.

The pattern of ratings indicates that personalization primarily shifts evaluations away from neutrality toward more positive judgments across significance, trustworthiness, and clarity. Across metrics, neutral/midpoint ratings (level 3) decreased for AI descriptions (significance: 27.1\% to 23.1\%; trust: 33.3\% to 27.4\%; clarity: 28.8\% to 21.4\%), alongside increases in higher-end ratings (e.g., trust level 5: 10.6\% to 14.1\%; clarity level 5: 13.1\% to 17.2\%). Trust is a critical factor in travel decision-making; notably, the lowest trust rating (level 1) halved from 1.6\% to 0.8\%. A plausible mechanism is that aligning emphasis with stated interests increases perceived relevance while keeping underlying facts stable; similarly, highlighting reader-relevant details may make the same information easier to process, improving clarity without necessarily changing content.

Generalizability is limited by the sample, which was predominantly young and educated (mean age 30.94 years), with many students and engineers, and primarily Indian and German participants; responses may differ for older users, less tech-familiar groups, or other cultural contexts. For tourism platforms, the results suggest that LLMs can tailor emphasis in POI descriptions based on onboarding inputs or inferred preferences while maintaining perceived trust and improving clarity. Given the sensitivity of trust, any disclosure of AI involvement should be designed to set appropriate expectations without unnecessarily eroding credibility.

\subsection{Limitations}
Several limitations should be acknowledged. First, participants
evaluated descriptions in a controlled study environment rather
than during actual trip planning, which may not fully capture realworld decision-making contexts. Second, the study did not track
whether personalized descriptions influenced actual visit intentions
or behaviors beyond stated preferences. Third, the within-subjects
design, while enabling direct comparisons, may have introduced
demand characteristics or heightened attention to differences between versions. Fourth, the study did not examine potential negative effects of over-personalization, such as filter bubbles or reduced
serendipitous discovery of POIs outside user-stated interests.
