\section{Conclusion}

This study provides preliminary evidence that large language models can personalize POI descriptions in ways that may improve how users judge travel information. In our sample, AI-adapted descriptions received higher ratings for both clarity and trustworthiness, while perceived significance remained comparable. Optional post-study feedback suggests that participants were generally comfortable reading AI-generated descriptions in this setting.

These findings suggest that personalization may support travel decision-making by foregrounding details that match user interests while preserving core facts about a place. For travel platforms, LLM-based rewriting may offer a path toward scaling tailored micro-content without extensive manual editing. More broadly, these initial results point toward the possibility that description framing could shape perceived information quality alongside which places are recommended.
