\section{Conclusion}


This study shows that large language models can personalize point-of-interest descriptions in ways that improve how users read and judge travel information. Compared to non-personalized text, the AI-adapted descriptions were generally perceived as clearer, and they also tended to be seen as more trustworthy. Participants also reported feeling comfortable engaging with AI-generated content in this setting. 

These findings suggest that personalization can help people make more confident decisions by foregrounding details that match their interests while preserving the core facts of a place. For travel platforms, LLM-based rewriting offers a practical path to scaling tailored micro-content without relying on extensive manual editorial effort. More broadly, the study highlights that how a place is described can be as influential as which place is recommended for creating a relevant and engaging user experience.
