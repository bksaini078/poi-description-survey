\section{Methodology}
This section outlines the methodology employed to evaluate the effectiveness of LLM-generated personalized POI descriptions compared to original manual descriptions. We describe the study design, description personalization process, evaluation interface, metrics, procedure, and participant recruitment.

\subsection{Study Design}
A within-subjects experimental design was employed in which each participant evaluated both original and AI-personalized descriptions for multiple POIs. This design enabled direct comparison between description types while controlling for individual differences in preferences and expectations. The study presented participants with 10 POIs spanning five categories: hotels, restaurants, museums, gardens, and landmarks. Each category included two POIs to ensure diverse representation of travel-related content. POIs were selected to include both internationally recognized locations and lesser-known destinations, providing variety in familiarity levels.

\subsection{Description Personalization}
For each POI, two description versions were prepared. The original description was sourced from official tourism websites and travel platforms, representing standard, non-personalized content. The AI-adapted version was generated using a large language model (GPT-4) that received the original description along with each participant's stated interests, hobbies, and travel preferences as input. The LLM was instructed to adapt the description to emphasize aspects most relevant to the user's profile while maintaining factual accuracy and the core information about the POI. For example, a museum description for a participant interested in history would emphasize historical significance and artifacts, while the same museum described for a family traveler would highlight interactive exhibits and educational programs.

\subsection{Evaluation Interface}
Participants viewed descriptions through a web-based interface that displayed two versions side by side, labeled as Version A and Version B (see Figure~\ref{fig:eval-interface}). The interface did not indicate which version was AI-generated. Each POI was accompanied by a representative image to provide visual context. The presentation order of AI and manual descriptions was randomized across POIs to prevent order bias. Participants could not proceed to the next POI without completing evaluations for the current one, ensuring complete data collection.

\subsection{Evaluation Metrics}
Participants evaluated each description on three core dimensions using Likert scales:

\textbf{Significance:} "Does the description effectively communicate the significance and offerings of the place?" rated on a 5-point scale from Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree. This metric assessed whether the description conveyed the value and appeal of the POI.

\textbf{Trustworthiness:} "How trustworthy does this description appear to be?" rated on a 5-point scale from Not at all to Extremely. This metric measured perceived credibility and reliability of the information.

\textbf{Clarity:} "How clear and complete is the information provided?" rated on a 5-point scale from Very Unclear to Very Clear. This metric evaluated information quality and comprehensibility.

After rating both descriptions individually, participants completed comparative evaluations selecting which description was more engaging, more informative, more appealing, and more likely to encourage them to visit. Participants also reported their familiarity with each POI using three categories: visited in person, heard about it, or never heard of it.

\subsection{Procedure}
The study was conducted in three phases using a secure web platform. First, participants provided informed consent and completed a survey regarding their demographics, travel styles, and interests. This data was used to personalize the AI-generated descriptions. Second, participants evaluated 10 POIs sequentially by reading both description versions and rating them on core metrics and familiarity. This evaluation phase lasted approximately 20 to 30 minutes. Finally, participants provided overall feedback on their experience and their comfort level with AI-generated travel content. All data were collected anonymously to ensure privacy and ethical compliance.

\subsection{Participants}
A total of 51 participants were recruited through personal networks via email and direct messaging. Participants were required to be at least 18 years old and possess basic English proficiency to ensure effective engagement with POI descriptions. No specific travel experience was mandated, as the study aimed to assess AI-generated descriptions across diverse user segments.

The sample demographics were as follows. Ages ranged from 22 to 49 years (M = 30.94, SD = 6.74). Gender distribution included 34 males (67\%) and 17 females (33\%). Nationalities were predominantly Indian (n = 28) and German (n = 21), with minimal Chinese and Iranian representation. Marital status comprised 25 married, 22 single, 3 in relationships, and 1 divorced. Professional backgrounds centered on students (n = 22) and engineers (n = 16), with smaller numbers of academics, healthcare professionals, and public service workers.

Travel experience levels showed intermediate (n = 21) and experienced (n = 19) travelers forming the majority, with 9 beginners and 2 experts. This distribution provided a range of perspectives on travel content while maintaining concentration in engaged, technology-familiar demographics.
