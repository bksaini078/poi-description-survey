\section{Results}
This section presents findings from the user study comparing LLM-adapted POI descriptions against manual baselines. Across 51 participants evaluating 10 POIs each, the study collected 510 paired ratings per metric (manual versus AI). Paired statistical tests were employed to account for the within-subjects design, where each participant evaluated both description types for the same POI. Table~\ref{tab:paired_manual_ai_full} summarizes mean ratings and statistical outcomes, while Figure~\ref{fig:mean-ratings} visualizes mean differences with 95\% confidence intervals.

\paragraph{Perceived Significance.}
Both description types received favorable ratings, and the difference was not statistically significant ($p = .154$, $d = 0.08$). This suggests that LLM-based adaptation maintained perceived significance without diminishing users' understanding of what each location offers. The result suggests that personalization did not appear to distort or weaken the core message about a POI's importance and unique characteristics.
\vspace{-0.8em}
\paragraph{Trustworthiness.}
AI-generated descriptions received higher trustworthiness ratings ($p < .001$, $d = 0.19$). This result is interesting given common concerns about AI-generated content credibility~\cite{li_generative_2025}. One possible explanation is that personalization may have enhanced perceived relevance by highlighting aspects aligned with participants' stated interests, potentially contributing to perceptions of accuracy and reliability.
\vspace{-0.8em}
\paragraph{Clarity and Completeness.}
AI-generated descriptions were rated significantly higher on clarity ($p = .002$, $d = 0.17$). This finding aligns with the personalization goal: descriptions that emphasize details matching user interests may be easier to read and understand. By focusing on relevant information, personalized descriptions may reduce the effort required to extract useful content, potentially helping users process the same facts more efficiently.

\subsection{Comparative Preferences}
Beyond individual ratings, participants completed comparative preference questions, selecting which description they found more engaging. Responses were nearly evenly distributed: 221 selections (43.3\%) favored AI-generated descriptions, 220 selections (43.1\%) favored manual descriptions, and 69 selections (13.5\%) indicated both versions were equally engaging. This parity suggests that AI-adapted descriptions achieved comparable engagement to manual descriptions in our sample. Participants did not appear to perceive the AI-adapted content as less interesting or appealing, suggesting that personalization may improve clarity and trustworthiness without compromising subjective engagement.

\subsection{Overall Study Feedback}
A subset of 17 participants completed optional feedback ratings (5-point scale). Those who provided feedback were demographically similar to those who did not. Overall experience received positive ratings ($\mu = 3.88$, $\sigma = 0.70$). Participants expressed moderate support for automatic POI adaptation ($\mu = 3.75$, $\sigma = 0.93$) and high comfort with AI-generated travel content ($\mu = 3.94$, $\sigma = 0.83$), suggesting receptiveness to AI-generated descriptions when demonstrating quality and relevance.

While perceived significance remained comparable between conditions, AI-adapted descriptions achieved statistically significant improvements in both trustworthiness and clarity. These gains occurred without sacrificing engagement, as comparative preferences showed no systematic bias toward either description type. The results suggest that LLM-based personalization can enhance perceived information quality in tourism micro-content by tailoring emphasis to user interests while preserving factual accuracy and reader engagement.
